1. THE INBOUND HOOK (The Primer)
The Mechanic: The very first words seen as the user scrolls into the NaviCue. Fades out quickly.

The Throttle: Max 8 words. heading.h3.

The Purpose: To bridge the psychological gap between the last NaviCue and this one.

Example: "The mind is spinning a new trap."

2. THE NARRATIVE CANOPY (The Core Frame)
The Mechanic: The top-anchored, readable text block that greets the user. It dictates the entire context of the silent physics below. Collapses the millisecond the screen is touched.

The Throttle: Max 40 words. body.large.

The Purpose: The Why. It tells the user what the unlabeled mass in the center actually represents today.

Example: "You are carrying the expectation of an entire room. Notice how incredibly dense this feels. We do not need to absorb it. Step to the side and let it fall."

3. THE SEMANTIC PILL (The Collapsed State)
The Mechanic: When the user touches the glass and the Canopy collapses upward, it shrinks into a tiny, elegant, pill-shaped UI element at the top dead-center of the screen.

The Throttle: Max 2 words. ui.caption.

The Purpose: It acts as the "re-toggle" button, but it also replaces the old Kinetic Tag. Since the block itself is unlabeled, the Pill softly reminds the user what they are interacting with.

Example: THE EXPECTATION or THE NOISE

4. THE AMBIENT SUBTEXT (The Subconscious)
The Mechanic: Blurred, 8% opacity text living in the deep Z-axis background. It scrolls via parallax and never interacts with the physics.

The Throttle: Max 6 words.

The Purpose: To validate the user's underlying anxiety without forcing them to read it.

Example: (blurred in background) "i have to be perfect"

5. THE IDLE WHISPER (The Kinesthetic Nudge)
The Mechanic: Only appears at the bottom of the screen (opacity: 0 to 0.5) if the user has touched the physics but hasn't resolved the puzzle within 4 seconds.

The Throttle: Max 4 words. Lowercase.

The Purpose: Replaces the static CTA. It provides a gentle, verb-driven hint.

Example: "swipe to redirect" or "hold the center"

6. THE THRESHOLD MORPH (The Physics Resolution)
The Mechanic: This is pure magic. The exact millisecond the user solves the physics puzzle (e.g., the block shatters, the wave passes), the tiny Semantic Pill at the top of the screen mathematically scrambles and crossfades into a new word.

The Throttle: Matches the character count of the Semantic Pill.

The Purpose: It provides a micro-hit of dopamine and semantic resolution without interrupting the visual flow.

Example: The Pill says THE NOISE. The user clears the physics. The Pill flips to THE SIGNAL.

7. THE OUTBOUND RECEIPT (The Handoff)
The Mechanic: As the user begins to scroll to the next NaviCue, this final thought fades into the center of the screen, stamping the neural pathway before the transition.

The Throttle: Max 10 words.

The Purpose: The final philosophical lock.

Example: "Untouchable. Keep moving."
