ATOM 11: THE WAVE COLLAPSE ENGINE (Probability to Reality)

The Intent: Anxiety is a probability cloud—a million terrible things that might happen. The Sovereign Mind knows that observing the data forces it to collapse into a single, manageable reality.
The Physics: Gaussian blur interpolation, procedural noise reduction, and focal crystallization.
Implementations from the Master File:

The Wave Collapse: A cloud of blur represents infinite probability. Each tap or sustained hold mathematically sharpens the cloud into a single, defined particle of truth.

The Raw Data: A simulated camera feed that is entirely blurry until the user anchors their attention, forcing the lens to resolve.
The Front-End Mandate: Use CSS backdrop-filter: blur() layered over WebGL particle clouds. The haptic signature starts as a wide, airy, diffuse static that rapidly condenses into a single, sharp, crystalline ping as the blur reaches 0px.

The Front-End Mandate: Two distinct UI states (e.g., a red UI and a blue UI) rapidly rapidly oscillating or blending via CSS mix-blend-mode. The haptics mimic a frantic coin toss that suddenly slaps flat onto the glass.

ATOM 16: THE UNCERTAINTY BLUR ENGINE (The Precision Trap)

ATOM 17: THE QUANTUM TUNNEL ENGINE (Permeable Barriers)

The Intent: To prove that seemingly impossible, 100% solid barriers actually contain microscopic space. You do not always have to break a wall; sometimes you can just pass through it.
The Physics: Collision detection with a 5% "ghost/pass-through" variable.
Implementations from the Master File:

The Quantum Tunnel: A solid, heavy barrier blocks the user's glowing node. If they smash into it fast, it acts like brick. If they gently, persistently press the node directly into the wall, the physics engine glitches, allowing the node to miraculously slip right through the atomic gaps.
The Front-End Mandate: Use Matter.js. Set the wall to be a static body. If the user's applied force is below a specific, incredibly low threshold for 3 seconds, temporarily disable collision detection and let the element slide through.
