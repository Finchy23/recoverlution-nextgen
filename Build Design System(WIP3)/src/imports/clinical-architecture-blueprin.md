FORM: THE CLINICAL PHYSICS
(The Semantic Payloads for Deep Integration)
The Principle of Dual Awareness
When a therapist guides a patient through trauma, their primary job is to keep the patient in their "Window of Tolerance." If the patient gets too triggered, they dissociate. The therapist uses their voice, eye contact, or tapping to keep the patient anchored in the present room while their mind accesses the past.
The Recoverlution UI must act as that therapist. It must maintain Dual Awareness. The Figma agent must never design a screen where the schema/trigger is the only thing the user feels or sees. There must always be a structural, somatic anchor on the glass holding them to the present moment.
1. THE CLINICAL CONTAINERS (The Therapist-Approved Payload)
The backend JSON schema will feed these exact containers. The Figma agent needs to design the typography and WebGL to reflect the slow, deliberate physics of actual trauma integration:
* THE RESOURCE (The Vagal Brake)
    * The Clinical Function: Before any heavy lifting begins, the nervous system must be resourced.
    * The UI Shape: A continuous, rhythmic haptic pulse and a slow-breathing visual anchor at the base of the glass. It does not demand reading; it demands entrainment. This runs continuously under the entire practice.
* THE TITRATION (The Controlled Exposure)
    * The Clinical Function: Introducing the schema (e.g., "I am fundamentally broken") not as an overwhelming screen takeover, but as a contained, observable object.
    * The UI Shape: The text is heavily constrained by a typographic bounding box. It sits in the midground of the Z-axis. It is visible, but the UI specifically prevents it from dominating the visual hierarchy.
* THE PENDULATION (The Somatic Shift)
    * The Clinical Function: The core mechanism of Somatic Experiencing. Moving the user's attention from the friction (The Titration) back to the safety (The Resource), training the nervous system that it can visit the pain and return safely.
    * The UI Shape: A physical mechanic. The UI prompts the user to drag their thumb between the schema and the glowing anchor. As they slide away from the text, the text blurs, and the haptic heartbeat strengthens.
* THE DEFUSION (The Decoupling)
    * The Clinical Function: The core of ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy). Teaching the user that they are not the thought; they are the observer of the thought. We do not destroy the text; we change the user's relationship to it.
    * The UI Shape: The user presses the schema. Instead of shattering, the text literally detaches from the foreground and drifts deep into the Z-axis background. It becomes small, ambient, and powerless, but it is still there. The user watches it drift.
* THE SOMATIC WASH (The Integration)
    * The Clinical Function: The discharge of autonomic energy. Closing the loop so the user doesn't leave the app dysregulated.
    * The UI Shape: The typography slowly fades into a full-screen, bimodal visual and acoustic wave. A sweeping, parasympathetic clearing of the glass.

1. The "Rescue Mission" (Imagery Rescripting / Schema Therapy)
The exact technique your friend experienced. Used to heal early attachment wounds, grief, and childhood trauma.
* The Clinical Mechanism: The brain processes vividly imagined experiences using the exact same neural pathways as real experiences. By bringing the capable "Adult Self" back to rescue the "Child Self" in a memory, the brain reconsolidates the memory with a feeling of safety instead of helplessness.
* The Digital Translation:
    * The UI: The screen drops to a deep, dark ambient mesh. A masterful bimodal audio track guides the user back to the origin of the schema.
    * The Somatic Anchor: When the user visualizes their younger self in the memory, the OS requires them to place both thumbs on the glass. The UI responds by generating a glowing, protective physical boundary (a shield) around the center of the screen.
    * The Re-Wire: The audio instructs: "You are the adult now. Hold the perimeter. Keep them safe." The haptics pulse with a slow, strong, grounded heartbeat. The user is physically acting out the protection they lacked.
2. The "Unburdening" (Internal Family Systems / IFS)
Used to heal self-sabotage, the Inner Critic, and deep shame.
* The Clinical Mechanism: IFS teaches that you are not one single mind; you are made of "parts." You are not angry; a part of you is angry to protect you. Healing happens when you physically separate your core "Self" from the burdened "Part," look at it, and ask it to step back.
* The Digital Translation:
    * The UI: The user identifies the overwhelming feeling (e.g., crushing anxiety). The UI generates a chaotic, jagged, dense mass of WebGL matter perfectly in the center of the glass.
    * The Somatic Anchor: The audio guides the user: "You are not this anxiety. It is just a part of you trying to keep you safe. Ask it to step back." * The Re-Wire: The UI unlocks. The user must literally press their thumb onto the chaotic mass and drag it out of the center, placing it on the periphery of the screen. The center of the glass immediately clears, glowing with a calm, smooth light. The user physically experiences the relief of "separation."
3. Bilateral Stimulation (EMDR - Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing)
The gold standard for acute trauma and PTSD. Used to downgrade the emotional intensity of a trapped memory.
* The Clinical Mechanism: When a user holds a traumatic memory in their mind while simultaneously experiencing alternating left-right stimulation (eye movements, tapping, or audio), it overloads the working memory. The brain is forced to "blur" the trauma, stripping it of its severe emotional charge.
* The Digital Translation:
    * The UI: The user is asked to hold the heavy memory. The UI generates a single, stark focal node that sweeps smoothly from the far left edge of the glass to the far right, across the Z-axis.
    * The Somatic Anchor: The OS utilizes Spatial Audio and alternating haptics. As the visual node hits the left side of the screen, the left earphone chimes and the left side of the phone vibrates. Then the right. Left, right, left, right.
    * The Re-Wire: The user follows the node with their eyes while the bilateral physics hijack the nervous system, rapidly cooling down the amygdala and safely archiving the memory.
4. Titration & Discharge (Somatic Experiencing / Peter Levine)
Used to release trapped physical tension and "freeze" responses. Pure physiological healing.
* The Clinical Mechanism: Trauma isn't in the event; it's trapped in the nervous system. When an animal survives an attack, it literally shakes the adrenaline out of its body. Humans suppress it. Somatic Experiencing asks the user to locate the physical shape of the anxiety in their body and allow it to discharge.
* The Digital Translation:
    * The UI: The audio asks the user where the friction lives in their body (e.g., a tight knot in the chest). The OS asks them to draw the boundary of that feeling on the glass. The user traces a tight, dense circle.
    * The Somatic Anchor: The UI solidifies that drawn shape into a heavy digital mass. The audio instructs the user to breathe into the edges of it.
    * The Re-Wire: As the user breathes, the UI physics slowly allow the tightly drawn mass to expand, lose its sharp edges, and diffuse into the ambient background. The user watches the visual representation of their physical pain slowly evaporate, triggering an actual autonomic exhale.
