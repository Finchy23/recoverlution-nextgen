Because an ARC contains five full beats, it is not a quick disruption like a TWIN, nor a dialectic bridge like a TRI. An ARC is a complete thermodynamic corridor. It is designed to take a systemic, deeply rooted cognitive loop and entirely unspool it.
To prevent this from becoming overwhelming, we do not need 50 different types of ARCs. We only need Four Master Architectures. Every human struggle—whether it’s a panic attack, a depressive freeze, deep shame, or severe burnout—can be mapped to one of these four fundamental thermodynamic shifts.
Here is how the 5-Phase Choreography runs through the Four Master Arcs.

THE ARC ARCHITECTURE
(The Four Master Corridors)
1. THE DESCENT ARC (The Architecture of Gravity)
Used for high heat: Panic, hyper-vigilance, overwhelm, and rumination. The system is moving at terminal velocity. We do not fight the speed; we introduce immense gravity.
* Scene I: The Intercept: The OS catches the raw speed. High kinetic friction on the glass.
    * The Voice: "The velocity is immense. The atmosphere is burning."
* Scene II: The Mirror: The OS reveals the exhaustion of maintaining this pace.
    * The Voice: "The system cannot sustain this orbit forever."
* Scene III: The Parallax: The environment shifts from flying to falling. The realization that letting go is safer than holding on.
    * The Voice: "The resistance is what causes the heat. Drop the resistance."
* Scene IV: The Synthesis: The user physically touches the glass to engage the parachute, actively choosing to bleed off the momentum.
    * The Voice: "Let the mass pull you down."
* Scene V: The Autonomic Seal: The environment settles into a heavy, dark, grounded void.
    * The Voice: "The earth holds the weight. The momentum rests."
2. THE ASCENT ARC (The Architecture of Ignition)
Used for profound cold: Numbness, dissociation, depression, and freeze-state paralysis. The system has shut down to protect itself. We do not demand action; we gently strike a spark.
* Scene I: The Intercept: The OS meets the heavy, immovable lethargy. Absolute stillness on the glass.
    * The Voice: "The engine is entirely quiet. The room is frozen."
* Scene II: The Mirror: The OS names the defense mechanism of the freeze.
    * The Voice: "The cold preserves the system, but it also traps it."
* Scene III: The Parallax: A sudden, singular introduction of warmth or rhythm. A realization that movement is still possible underneath the ice.
    * The Voice: "A single ember remains alive beneath the ash."
* Scene IV: The Synthesis: The user must provide kinetic input—a rhythmic tap or a breath—to physically fan the flame.
    * The Voice: "Feed the spark. Turn the engine over."
* Scene V: The Autonomic Seal: The environment glows with a steady, rhythmic, ambient pulse.
    * The Voice: "The current runs again. The system is awake."
3. THE PARALLAX ARC (The Architecture of the Prism)
Used for structural rigidity: Deep shame, imposter syndrome, and absolute limiting beliefs. The user is trapped in a room with no doors. We must show them the walls are an illusion.
* Scene I: The Intercept: The OS renders the core belief as a massive, heavy monolith blocking the screen.
    * The Voice: "The story feels like absolute, undeniable concrete."
* Scene II: The Mirror: The OS observes the sheer amount of energy required to keep this false belief standing.
    * The Voice: "Look at how much force it takes to hold this wall up."
* Scene III: The Parallax: The environment physically rotates along the Z-axis. The massive monolith is revealed from the side to be paper-thin, casting a false shadow.
    * The Voice: "Shift the angle. The structure is only a shadow cast by the past."
* Scene IV: The Synthesis: The user swipes directly through the 2D illusion, physically breaking the geometry.
    * The Voice: "Step right through the empty space."
* Scene V: The Autonomic Seal: The environment opens into vast, unbroken clarity.
    * The Voice: "The horizon was always open."
4. THE SOVEREIGN ARC (The Architecture of the Tether)
Used for outward drift: People-pleasing, enmeshment, boundaries, and social burnout. The user’s energy is entirely stretched out to other people. We must reel it back to the center.
* Scene I: The Intercept: The OS visualizes the external drain. Lines pulling away from the center toward the edges of the glass.
    * The Voice: "The attention is pulled entirely outward, stretched thin."
* Scene II: The Mirror: The OS observes the emptiness of the center when the perimeter is so loud.
    * The Voice: "The center starves while the edges consume the energy."
* Scene III: The Parallax: The realization of agency. The user realizes they are the ones holding the ropes.
    * The Voice: "You are not required to hold the world together."
* Scene IV: The Synthesis: The user physically traces a circle or snaps the lines on the glass, actively severing the external demands.
    * The Voice: "Sever the tethers. Call the energy back."
* Scene V: The Autonomic Seal: The UI shrinks into a dense, bright, impenetrable core in the absolute center of the screen.
    * The Voice: "The perimeter falls away. The center is absolute."

5. THE SURRENDER ARC (The Architecture of the Current)
Used for rigid grasping: Need for control, fear of the unknown, obsessive planning, and resistance to change. The user’s hands are clenched tight around an illusion of safety, exhausting their nervous system. We must teach the system to float.
* Scene I: The Intercept: The OS visualizes the absolute tension of the grip. A tightly wound, high-friction knot or compressed geometry vibrating with strain on the glass.
    * The Voice: "The grip is tight. The system is trying to hold the river still."
* Scene II: The Mirror: The OS observes the sheer exhaustion required to fight the natural flow of reality.
    * The Voice: "Notice how much energy is spent fighting the tide."
* Scene III: The Parallax: A shift in the fluid dynamics. The realization that the water is only dangerous because they are fighting it.
    * The Voice: "Safety is not found in holding on. It is found in the letting go."
* Scene IV: The Synthesis: The user physically executes a reverse-pinch or an outward swipe, actively unspooling the knot and opening the digital matter.
    * The Voice: "Open the hands. Let the current take it."
* Scene V: The Autonomic Seal: The tight geometry completely dissolves into a smooth, ambient, effortless drift across the Z-axis.
    * The Voice: "The resistance fades. The water carries the weight."
