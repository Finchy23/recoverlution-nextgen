Here is the modular breakdown of the Sentient Canvas.

LAYER 1: ATMOSPHERE (The Light & Depth)
This dictates the color signature, the volumetric lighting, and the depth of the void behind the glass. It sets the emotional safety of the room.
1. The Sanctuary (Amber Hearth): Thick, volumetric shadows illuminated by a slowly pulsing, warm golden light (#E8A64C) that wraps the edges of the screen. It feels like a protective womb for vulnerable moments.
2. The Cryo-Chamber (Neural Reset): A crisp, ultra-clean environment using the neural-reset signature. A sub-surface cyan glow (#00CCE0) shines through the dark frosted glass, visually cooling a sympathetic nervous system spike.
3. The Abyssal Void: Absolute, pure solid.base black (#0F0D1A) with infinite negative space and zero texture. Used to instantly kill cognitive overwhelm by providing a visual sensory deprivation tank.
4. The Bioluminescent Mesh: Utilizing the void-presence signature. The darkness is underlaid with a faint, interconnected 3D neural constellation that glows softly, representing the user's unified data spine.
5. The Chiaroscuro Spotlight: Dramatic, high-contrast shadows where a single, soft, cinematic shaft of light illuminates only the exact center of the background. It forces intense focus on the present moment.
6. The Twilight Shroud: A soft, transitionary environment using the twilight-shift signature. A blend of deep purple (#A89BFF) and subtle rose (rgba(255, 107, 138, 0.08)) that feels like dusk, used for downshifting into sleep or rest.

LAYER 2: MOTION (The Kinetics & Rhythm)
This dictates how the atmosphere behaves. We ban linear, snappy animations. Every motion variable is governed by human biology and structural physics.
1. The Somatic Breath: The environment dynamically expands outward and contracts inward, mathematically locked to exactly 5.5 breaths per minute (the resonant frequency of the human nervous system).
2. Viscous Unspooling: Elements in the background glide past one another continuously, moving as if submerged in a heavy, invisible fluid. It represents neuroplasticity—heavy matter slowly reorganizing itself.
3. The Pendulum Settle: When the atmosphere shifts, it does not stop abruptly. Utilizing the spring cubic-bezier curve, the light or geometry overshoots by 2 pixels and smoothly rocks into a perfectly centered halt, proving the digital matter has heavy physical weight.
4. The Defocus Dissolve: When tension leaves the system, the background elements do not slide away; they slowly blur and sink backward into the Z-axis (utilizing the 2000ms dissolve duration). It mimics dropping a heavy stone into a deep ocean.
5. The Cymatic Ripple: The background visualizes invisible frequencies, sending smooth, concentric rings radiating outward from the center, mimicking the way sound waves organize matter.
6. Tectonic Drift: Massive, geometric shadows in the deep background slide over one another at an incredibly slow, geological pace. It creates a feeling of unshakeable gravity and monumental scale.

LAYER 3: INTERACTION (The Sentient Response)
This dictates how the environment behind the glass reacts to the physical presence of the user. It is the "ghost in the machine."
1. Gyroscopic Parallax: The deep atmosphere is tied directly to the phone's physical hardware. As the user’s hand naturally breathes or shifts, the background lighting and depth shift on a 3D axis, proving the screen is a window into a physical space, not a flat painting.
2. Haptic Entrainment (The Anchor Pulse): The physical motor of the device is synchronized to the background. As the light breathes, the phone delivers a deep, resonant haptic pulse, allowing the user to feel the system's heartbeat through their skin.
3. Floating Particle Displacement: Microscopic, bioluminescent particles drift in the void. When the user places their thumb on the glass, the particles seamlessly part and orbit around the heat of their touch, proving the environment is "aware" of them.
4. Surface Refraction (The Glass Warp): When the user presses the screen, the underlying frosted_glass properties dynamically alter. The blur radius and ambient light mathematically warp around the fingertip, making the UI feel like liquid slate.
5. Acoustic Flutter: When used in the Talk corridor, the background lighting acts as an active listener. The ambient glow faintly flickers and expands in real-time, matching the exact cadence and volume of the user's voice note.
6. Viscous Drag (Friction Resistance): When the user interacts with the glass, the background does not follow their finger perfectly at a 1:1 speed. It resists slightly, creating a micro-second of tension before catching up, mimicking the drag of moving through heavy water or thick clay.
