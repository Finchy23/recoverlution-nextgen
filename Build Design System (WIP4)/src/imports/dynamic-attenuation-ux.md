THE LAW OF ATTENTIONAL PHYSICS
(How the Glass Protects the Mind)
1. Dynamic Depth of Field (The Focal Lock)
In photography, when you want the subject to stand out, you open the aperture to blur the background. We do the exact same thing using the Z-axis of the Universal Player.

When the OS is in a resting or transition state, the background is sharp and active. But the exact millisecond a "Hero" payload (an Article, a NaviCue, a Practice) arrives on the glass, the OS applies a section_depth modifier.

The Mechanic: If the user opens an Insight, the glass immediately thickens from a surface (0px blur) to a floor state (4px blur, 0.8 overlay opacity).

The Result: The glowing Constellation or the moving Tectonic Drift doesn't disappear; it defocuses into a soft, ambient, ambient aura. The text remains razor-sharp in the foreground. The brain instantly knows exactly where to look without feeling like the environment died.

2. Kinetic Subordination (Motion Yielding)
The human eye is biologically hardwired to track motion (it is a survival reflex). If the background is moving while the user is trying to read or solve a puzzle, the visual cortex is under attack.

The Mechanic: The background must yield its kinetic energy to the foreground. When an active intervention is on the screen, the background motion parameters drop to the absolute minimum.

The Result: If the user is dragging a heavy digital mass in a NaviCue, the background Somatic Breath animation stops breathing and "holds its breath" along with the user. It restricts its movement to the 10-second masterBreath cycle, making it practically imperceptible unless you stare at it.

3. Room-Specific Attenuation (How it applies to the 3 main states)
We must tune this foreground/background relationship specifically for the cognitive load of the room the user is in.

A. The "Read" State (Insights & Articles)
The Goal: Parasympathetic Absorption.

The Conundrum: Reading requires maximum prefrontal cortex focus. Background distractions are fatal here.

The Solution: The background drops to the panel frosted glass setting (blur 24px, dark background). The background elements become completely static. The interaction variables (like Gyroscopic Parallax or Floating Particles) are disabled. The background becomes a static, heavily blurred painting to perfectly support the player.body typography.

B. The "Action" State (NaviCues)
The Goal: Bottom-Up Autonomic Regulation.

The Conundrum: The user must interact with a physical puzzle on the glass, but the background is also trying to regulate them.

The Solution: Harmonic Fusion. The background and the foreground atom link their physics. If the user is supposed to drag a heavy block downward to downshift their heart rate, the background Cryo-Chamber light does not do its own thing—it dims and brightens in perfect 1:1 synchronization with the user's thumb movement on the block. The background becomes the lighting engine for the atom.

C. The "Talk" State (The Guided Corridor)
The Goal: Bounded Reflection.

The Conundrum: The user is typing or speaking, which is vulnerable. If the UI is too static, it feels like a cold chatbot.

The Solution: The background remains sharp, but it goes dark. We activate the Acoustic Flutter interaction. The only thing moving in the background is the soft Amber Resonance glow, pulsing gently to the rhythm of their voice note. The UI atoms (the Talk prompts) sit cleanly on top, while the background provides active, non-distracting empathy.

THE RULE OF THUMB FOR THE DESIGN DESK
To prevent the design from getting too noisy, enforce this absolute rule: The Total Energy Equation.

The screen has a total energy budget of 100%.

If the user is on the Home/Stream view (just looking at their Constellation), the Background gets 90% of the energy, and the UI gets 10%.

If the user opens an Article, the UI (Typography) demands 90% of the energy. Therefore, the Background must instantly drop to 10% (blurring out, darkening, stopping its motion).

By using the sectionDepth blur modifiers and Kinetic Subordination, the Universal Player breathes like a lung—pushing the environment forward when the user needs to feel held, and pulling it deep into the dark glass when the user needs to focus.
