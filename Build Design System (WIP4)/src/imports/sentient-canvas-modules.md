THE LAW OF ATTENTIONAL PHYSICS
(How the Glass Protects the Mind)
1. Dynamic Depth of Field (The Focal Lock)
In photography, when you want the subject to stand out, you open the aperture to blur the background. We do the exact same thing using the Z-axis of the Universal Player.
When the OS is in a resting or transition state, the background is sharp and active. But the exact millisecond a "Hero" payload (an Article, a NaviCue, a Practice) arrives on the glass, the OS applies a section_depth modifier.
The Mechanic: If the user opens an Insight, the glass immediately thickens from a surface (0px blur) to a floor state (4px blur, 0.8 overlay opacity).
The Result: The glowing Constellation or the moving Tectonic Drift doesn't disappear; it defocuses into a soft, ambient, ambient aura. The text remains razor-sharp in the foreground. The brain instantly knows exactly where to look without feeling like the environment died.
2. Kinetic Subordination (Motion Yielding)
The human eye is biologically hardwired to track motion (it is a survival reflex). If the background is moving while the user is trying to read or solve a puzzle, the visual cortex is under attack.
The Mechanic: The background must yield its kinetic energy to the foreground. When an active intervention is on the screen, the background motion parameters drop to the absolute minimum.
The Result: If the user is dragging a heavy digital mass in a NaviCue, the background Somatic Breath animation stops breathing and "holds its breath" along with the user. It restricts its movement to the 10-second masterBreath cycle, making it practically imperceptible unless you stare at it.
