## Purpose
- provide the first working answer set for Trust Chain family design without reopening route or doctrine planning

## Trust Chain truth
- `/product` explains what the system is
- `/method` explains how change happens
- `/proof` explains what makes it believable
- `/authority` explains why we have the right to speak
- together, they form one trust sequence

## Working answers

### Family posture
- calmer than Launch Spine
- more structured than Launch Spine
- more editorial than Launch Spine
- still unmistakably part of the same organism

### What these pages must do
- reduce the burden on the homepage
- let a serious reader move from intrigue to belief
- avoid dumping expert language as the main reading frame
- carry one dominant idea per section

### What these pages must not do
- become papers
- become internal doctrine pages
- become product docs in prettier shells
- create four separate aesthetics

### Visual answer
- stronger information hierarchy
- quieter media burden
- tighter section discipline
- recurring trust mechanisms instead of recurring decorative mechanics

### Motion answer
- motion should clarify sequence, relation, or depth
- motion should not compete with the page argument
- if a page cannot survive as still hierarchy, the system is wrong

### Copy answer
- consumer-facing first
- evidence and expert material support the page, not the other way around
- every section answers one question and moves the reader one step closer to belief

### Asset answer
- `mindform` and `vessel` should do most of the work here
- `state-flow` should support continuity and relief
- `organism` should appear only where it strengthens the page argument, not as a repeated signature

### Page-specific answers
#### `/product`
- the page should make the rooms and whole-system logic understandable
- it should not explain every room equally

#### `/method`
- the page should make change feel structural, not motivational
- it should not sound academic

#### `/proof`
- the page should make proof feel biological and accumulative
- it should not sound like a KPI dashboard

#### `/authority`
- the page should ground authority in reality, not institution theatre
- it should not sound superior or clinical for its own sake
